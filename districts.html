Where to go?


Caressed by Arabian Sea and Western Ghats, kerala has 14 districts, each of which has their own unique tourist destinations. Let's Explore...

Trivandrum

Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist centre, known for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the beaches of Kovalam and Varkala.

Kollam

People love visiting the Ashtamudi Backwaters along with the Thenmala Ecotourism Centre, Palaruvi Waterfalls and Jatayupara.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta district is known as the Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism, of the Kerala State.

Alapuzha

Alapuzha best known for houseboat cruises along the rustic Kerala backwaters, a network of tranquil canals and lagoons.

Kottayam

Kottayam is also called as “Akshara Nagari” which means the “city of letters” considering its contribution to print media and literature.

Idukki

Idukki district is famous for its rich flora and fauna. Idukki is one of the greenest districts of Kerala.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam has various historical sites, temples, forts, beaches, museums, and other tourist places to attract travelers.

Thrissur

Thrissur is well-known for its temples, religious sites, and beaches.There are a number of beautiful parks, waterfalls and more.

Palakkad

Palakkad or Palghat, situated in the foothills of the Nilgiris, is widely heralded as the gateway to Kerala.

Malappuram

Malappuram is famous for Kottakkunnu, Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary, Keralam Kundu Waterfalls and many more.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode is often recognized as a place where the famed explorer, Vasco Da Gama first set his foot in India.

Wayanad

Wayanad is famous for its large amount of camping and trekking trails, breathtaking waterfalls, caves and bird-watching sites.

Kannur

Kannur is famous for Politics and Temple festivals. The region is famous for the dazzling ritual performance called Theyyam.

Kasargod

Kasargod is a small coastal town in Northern Pary of Kerala displaying a delightful blend of various religions, culture and bounty of nature.
